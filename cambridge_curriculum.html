Cambridge Curriculum Guide

Cambridge IGCSE

Cambridge IGCSE (International General Certificate of Secondary Education) is the world's most popular international qualification for 14-16 year olds. It develops skills in:

  • Creative thinking
  • Inquiry and problem-solving
  • Application of knowledge to real-world scenarios
  • Subject content knowledge

Students typically take 8-10 IGCSE subjects.

Cambridge International AS & A Levels

These qualifications are for students aged 16-19 and are recognized by universities worldwide. Key features include:

  • Flexible pathways - AS Level can be taken as a standalone qualification or as part of the full A Level
  • In-depth subject exploration
  • Development of higher-order thinking skills
  • Independent research and analysis

Students typically take 3-4 A Level subjects.

Assessment

Cambridge assessments are primarily external examinations held in May/June and October/November each year. Grades range from A* to G for IGCSE and A* to E for AS and A Levels. Some subjects include coursework components or practical assessments.

Study Strategies

  • Understand the syllabus requirements thoroughly
  • Practice with past papers to understand question patterns
  • Focus on command terms in questions (e.g., "describe," "explain," "evaluate")
  • Develop strong time management for examinations
  • Create concise notes that highlight key concepts
  • Form study groups for collaborative learning
