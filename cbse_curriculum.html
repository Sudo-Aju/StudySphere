CBSE Curriculum Guide

Secondary Level (Classes 9-10)

CBSE offers a broad curriculum that includes:

  • Languages (English, Hindi, etc.)
  • Mathematics
  • Science (Physics, Chemistry, Biology)
  • Social Science (History, Geography, Political Science, Economics)
  • Additional subjects (IT, Arts, etc.)

The Board Examination at the end of Class 10 is a significant milestone in a student's academic journey.

Senior Secondary Level (Classes 11-12)

Students choose one of the following streams:

  • Science Stream: Physics, Chemistry, Mathematics/Biology, plus electives
  • Commerce Stream: Accountancy, Business Studies, Economics, Mathematics, plus electives
  • Humanities/Arts Stream: History, Geography, Political Science, Economics, plus electives

The Class 12 Board Examination results are crucial for university admissions.

Assessment

CBSE follows a Continuous and Comprehensive Evaluation (CCE) system that includes:

  • Formative Assessments (classroom participation, projects, assignments)
  • Summative Assessments (written examinations)
  • Practical examinations for subjects like Science and Computer Science

The grading system uses a 9-point scale from A1 (highest) to E2 (lowest).

Study Strategies

  • Follow NCERT textbooks thoroughly as they form the core of the curriculum
  • Practice with previous years' question papers
  • Create chapter-wise notes with important formulas and concepts
  • Focus on HOTS (Higher Order Thinking Skills) questions
  • Develop a structured study timetable that covers all subjects
  • Participate actively in practical work and projects
