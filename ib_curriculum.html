IB Curriculum Guide

The International Baccalaureate (IB) is a globally recognized educational program that aims to develop inquiring, knowledgeable, and caring young people. The IB offers four educational programs for students aged 3-19.

IB Programs

  • Primary Years Programme (PYP) - For students aged 3-12
  • Middle Years Programme (MYP) - For students aged 11-16
  • Diploma Programme (DP) - For students aged 16-19
  • Career-related Programme (CP) - For students aged 16-19

IB Diploma Programme Structure

The IB Diploma Programme is the most widely recognized IB qualification. It consists of:

  • Six subject groups: Studies in language and literature, Language acquisition, Individuals and societies, Sciences, Mathematics, The arts
  • Core components: Theory of Knowledge (TOK), Extended Essay (EE), Creativity, Activity, Service (CAS)

Students choose three subjects at Higher Level (HL) and three at Standard Level (SL).

Assessment

IB assessment includes both internal assessments (conducted by teachers) and external examinations. The final grade for each subject ranges from 1 (lowest) to 7 (highest). Students can earn up to 45 points total (42 from six subjects plus 3 from TOK and EE).

Study Strategies

  • Create a balanced study schedule that accounts for all subjects
  • Focus on understanding concepts rather than memorization
  • Practice past papers to familiarize yourself with the exam format
  • Form study groups to discuss complex topics
  • Start your Extended Essay and Internal Assessments early
  • Maintain a CAS portfolio throughout the program
